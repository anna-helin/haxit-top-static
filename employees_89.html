Background:
Sauron's original name was Mairon. He was the mightiest Maia of the Vala Aulë the Smith, and learned much from Aulë of smithing and handiwork, becoming a great craftsman and "mighty in the lore of" Aulë's people.[2] He was among the most powerful Maiar.In the Days before days, Mairon was as Eru had created him: good and noble. His greatest virtue was his love of order and perfection, and dislike of anything wasteful. However, this would also prove to be the cause of his downfall, as Mairon saw in Melkor the will and power that would help him achieve his personal ends quicker than if he pursued them alone. However, while Melkor wanted to either control or destroy Middle-earth itself, Mairon's desire was to dominate the minds and wills of its creatures for what he perceived to be their own benefit. To see his marvelous designs through, Mairon sought to increase his innate power; so he joined with Melkor, soon becoming his chief agent.
Strengths:
Physical Might: In his original form as a Maia, Sauron possessed immense physical strength and power. He was capable of transforming into various forms, including a formidable Dark Lord, which showcased his might. Control and Manipulation: Sauron demonstrated significant mental and magical strength, allowing him to corrupt and manipulate others. He seduced many, including the Elves and Men, leading them to create the Rings of Power. His ability to dominate minds and bend others to his will is a testament to his strength. Creation of the One Ring: One of Sauron's greatest feats was the forging of the One Ring. This act not only enhanced his own power but also allowed him to exert control over the other Rings of Power and their bearers. War of the Last Alliance: During the War of the Last Alliance, Sauron fought against the combined forces of Elves and Men. His ability to lead vast armies and engage in direct combat against powerful heroes like Gil-galad and Elendil showcased his martial strength.Resilience and Return: After his defeat at the end of the Second Age, Sauron's spirit endured, demonstrating his resilience. He returned in the Third Age, gradually rebuilding his power in Mordor, which illustrates his ability to recover and regain strength despite previous defeats. Influence over Mordor: Sauron’s strength is also reflected in his ability to dominate and corrupt the land of Mordor, transforming it into a realm of darkness and fear. He commanded legions of Orcs and other dark creatures, further showcasing his power. Direct Confrontation with Heroes: In "The Lord of the Rings," Sauron’s Eye is a constant presence, representing his ability to see and influence events across Middle-earth. His strength is not just physical but also in his pervasive influence and ability to instill fear.
Weaknesses:

Before he was corrupted and insnared by Melkor, he was serving under Aule the smith god, and naturally having great wit and intellect even among the maiar, had his own ‘great’ vision for the world, but when Melkor took him and he served as the dark lord’s greatest luitenant, his vision was re-carved with the influence of evil. Than after Melkor, now as Morgoth was casted into the void, Sauron renounced his allegiance (a possible weakness of loyalty, especially when you consider how Melkor was the actual embodiment of evil, and he poured his ‘evil essence’ partially into Sauron), Sauron still quickly cooked up evil again in Mordor, and eventaully we see there that he having served in the war of wrath, probably a hyper-chaotic petri-dish of a war.
